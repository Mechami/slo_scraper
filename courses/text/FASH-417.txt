I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
FASH 9 - Clothing Construction I
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Reading Level IV; English Level III; Math Level II
Transfer Status: CSU
34 hours Lecture
51 hours Lab

3 Unit(s)

This is a beginning course in clothing construction. Topics include pattern selection and layout,
selection of fashion fabrics and interfacings, seam finishes and design features. Students will develop
their sewing skills though the construction of garments and samples utilizing professional sewing
techniques.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Identify and record specific body measurements and use these to adjust a commercial pattern for
proper fit.
B. Read and interpret pattern instructions for the construction of a garment, then select the most
suitable fabric.
C. Demonstrate the proper use and function of clothing construction equipment for personal and
professional sewing.
D. Identify the appropriate clothing construction techniques used to create particular garments.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read the materials from class text on seams and seam finishes. Be prepared to identify
different seams and seam finishes on actual samples in class.
2. Read an article about fabric grainline and layout of garments. Apply this technique to your
sewing project.

B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a 2-3 page summary of the process needed to assemble the two selected garments
selected by the instructor.
2. Prepare a 3-4 page journal entry about your experience putting together a garment. Be
sure to discuss your successes and challenges.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Visit a local fabric store to research fabrics and trims for your project. Bring your fabrics
and trims to class.
2. Obtain and assemble a binder of basic construction techniques, such as seam finishes,
zippers, buttonholes, etc.
