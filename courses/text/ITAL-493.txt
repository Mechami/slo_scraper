I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
ITAL 1 - First Semester Italian
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Reading Level III; English Level III
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
68 hours Lecture

4 Unit(s)

This course is a study of beginning Italian emphasizing grammar, reading, writing and speaking. It
also introduces the student to various aspects of Italian culture. Students who have completed at least
one, but fewer than two years of high school Italian (or equivalent) with a letter grade of "A" or "B"
within one year of the present date, should register for Second Semester Italian.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. recognize and begin to imitate Italian pronunciation
B. comprehend basic Italian at the introductory level
C. speak basic Italian at the introductory level
D. write basic Italian at the introductory level
E. formulate arguments supporting the importance of second language acquisition
F. appraise elements of Italian culture

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read a diagram with statistics about recreational activities of Italian youth and describe
the information to a partner.
2. Read a short text about the climates of different regions of Italy. Afterward, create a table
with the most important information, such as high temperatures and precipitation.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a brief diary entry about the day's events, in order to practice the past tense.
2. Write a short message to a friend describing the classes you are taking and your favorite
subject.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Complete several "cloze" activities focused on accurate conjugation of verbs located in
the textbook or handout and bring to the next class.
2. Prepare outside of class an original skit based on a communicative context found in course
materials. Perform the skit in class.
