I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
COS 101 - Cosmetology I
Prerequisite(s): COS 100 or Permission of Instructor
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
30 hours Lecture
210 hours Lab

5 Unit(s)

This course is the second in a seven-course sequence. Students are introduced to the principles and
practices of infection control, anatomy and physiology, and chemistry. Emphasis will be placed upon
skin, nail, and hair structure and growth and disorders of hair, scalp, and nails. Students will continue
developing the skills and techniques required for all aspects of hair care, hair cutting, hair treatments,
makeup application, skin care and nail care and the safe, hygienic practice of Cosmetology
procedures. Additionally, students will continue developing skills in human relations, customer
satisfaction and the professional attitudes that are vital to success in the beauty industry.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Explain the importance of anatomy and physiology to the practice of Cosmetology.
B. Describe the structure and function of cells, tissues, and body systems.
C. Name and explain basic functions of all major body systems.
D. Describe the structure, composition and function of the skin.
E. Identify skin, hair, and scalp disorders and diseases and describe required treatments and
precautions.
F. Demonstrate and apply Universal Precautions and infection control.
G. Clean different implements and products using appropriate disinfection techniques.
H. Describe structure, composition and growth of nails.
I. List and describe various disorders and irregularities of the nails.
J. Recognize diseases of the nails that should not be treated in the salon.
K. Demonstrate basic techniques of nail care procedures with emphasis on nail structure.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. After reading about salon infractions on the Board of Barbering and Cosmetology website,
prepare a list of the three most common infractions and what salons can do to avoid these
infractions.
2. Go to the Board of Barbering and Cosmetology website (www.barbercosmo.ca.gov) and
review how many complaints were filed against salons. Summarize in a chart the number
of complaints and the reasons for the complaints.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Design a survey instrument that could be utilized by local salons to evaluate customer
satisfaction and retention.
2. Write a 1-2 page essay summarizing your findings from an interview with a product
representative from Redken, Dermalogica or Paul Mitchell.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Visit 2-3 different local salons for the same service (e.g. manicure). Without identifying
the salon, summarize your thoughts regarding cleanliness, professionalism and
satisfaction with the service.
2. Conduct a survey of local salons that focuses on what services are offered and the price
range for those services. Summarize your findings in a chart or data table.
