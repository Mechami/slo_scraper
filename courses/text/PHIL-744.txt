COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
PHIL 58 - Teaching About Religion
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Reading Level V; English Level IV
Transfer Status: CSU
51 hours Lecture

3 Unit(s)

An introduction to the Abrahamic religions of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, along with the major
religious traditions of India and Asia, within the context of a practical exploration of the moral, legal,
and educational issues raised in teaching about religions in public schools in the United States.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Define and recall the Constitutional principles in teaching about religions in public schools in
the United States.
B. Identify and summarize key philosophical terms in the analysis of world views.
C. Recognize, describe and distinguish between the major religious traditions of the world with
special emphasis on their expression in the United States.
D. Assess and evaluate specific moral and legal controversies arising from the teaching of religions
in the public schools in the United States.
