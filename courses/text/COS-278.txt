I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
COS 106 - Cosmetology VI
Prerequisite(s): COS 105 or Permission of Instructor
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
30 hours Lecture
210 hours Lab

5 Unit(s)

This course is a capstone course for the cosmetology sequence. Students are introduced to the
principles of successful salon business management, and demonstration of proper technique and
practice in all aspects of hair, skin and nail care, treatments and procedures. Emphasis will be placed
on mastery of the necessary Cosmetology concepts, principles and skills required to pass the
California State Licensing exam for Cosmetology. Students will be required to demonstrate mastery of
the required skills and techniques for all aspects of hair care, hair cutting, hair treatments, makeup
application, skin care and nail care and the safe, hygienic practice of Cosmetology procedures.
Additionally, students will continue developing skills in human relations, customer satisfaction and
the professional attitudes that are vital to success in the beauty industry. Students will spend 30 hours
in a classroom setting and 210 hours in laboratory activities. Upon completing this course, students are
expected to have a minimum of 1,600 hours of applied skill practice required by the State of
California Board of Barbering and Cosmetology.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Demonstrate proper techniques for various haircutting, care and treatment services with
minimum supervision.
B. Demonstrate proper techniques for all aspects of nail care and artificial nail services with
minimal supervision.
C. Demonstrate proper draping and safety procedures for all types of hair, nail, and skin services.
D. Explain successful salon business management techniques and procedures.
E. Demonstrate mastery of hair care skills and procedures required to obtain State Board License.
F. Demonstrate mastery of skills and knowledge necessary to pass the Mock Board Exam.
G. Explain the rules and regulations issued by the Board of Barbering and Cosmetology.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read 2 – 3 professional articles that focus on the safe application of chemicals to the hair,
skin and scalp. Be prepared to give a three minute presentation to the class on this topic.
2. Read about the use of electricity, lasers and oxygen in skin treatment procedures. Be
prepared to present your findings in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a 1-2 page essay about the safe use of electricity and lasers in skin correction and
treatment.
2. Write a 1-2 page essay citing the pros and cons of controversial ingredients used in
common cosmetic products such as: triclosan, para-aminobenzoic acid, toluene, and
formaldehyde.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Interview a salon owner in your area. Focus on the aspects of salon ownership that interest
you most. Prepare a short written summary of your findings.
2. Enroll in an online, product focused course in hairstyling from www.redken.com or
www.paulmitchell.com. Be prepared to present what you learn to the class.
