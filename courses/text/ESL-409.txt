I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
ESL 310 - Survival/Life Skills for ESL Students
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
85 hours Lecture

0 Unit(s)

This course provides a conversational approach to the learning of elementary English. It offers the
non-native English speaker practice in verbal communication with special emphasis on literacy and
life skills. The introduction to basic literacy skills reinforces spoken English. Unlimited repeats.
Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory Only. Open Entry/Open Exit.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. operate effectively in a language-learning classroom environment
B. utilize beginning literacy skills outside the classroom
C. practice learned listening and speaking skills
D. use appropriate language responses to every-day social contexts
E. recognize various cultural perspectives
F. apply learned life skills to real life situations
G. create an information binder for future reference
