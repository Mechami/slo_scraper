I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
ESL 231 - ESL Level 5-Academic
Prerequisite(s): ESL 230 or qualifying eligibility from the ESL Assessment.
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
51 hours Lecture

3 Unit(s)

This course develops a non-native English speaking student's academic language skills including
reading, writing, listening, and speaking skills. Vocabulary development and spelling are also
addressed. Reading skills are expanded from the literal to the critical. More complex grammatical
structures and writing beyond the sentence and paragraph level are also stressed. Pass/No Pass Only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. answer correctly short answer or essay questions with a minimum of grammatical, punctuation,
and structure error
B. develop lists of difficult vocabulary and spelling challenges
C. comprehend both literal and figurative text at the appropriate level
D. analyze and discuss a reading either orally or in written format
E. research, organize, compose, and present a short oral presentation on an assigned topic
F. critique an oral presentation by another student
G. apply note-taking strategies (from both lecture and text) to a content area course
H. recognize cultural differences between her/his personal culture and the academic culture
