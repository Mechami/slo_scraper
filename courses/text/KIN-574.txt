I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
KIN 68 - Volleyball I
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
17 hours Lecture
34 hours Lab

1.5 Unit(s)

This course provides instruction in the six basic skills of volleyball: passing, setting, hitting, serving,
blocking, and individual defense. Students will learn history, rules, etiquette, and safe play of
volleyball. The elements of team play and game strategies will be identified and introduced.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. demonstrate the basic skills of: passing, setting, hitting, serving, blocking and individual
defense.
B. recognize and demonstrate the transition from offense to defense during game play.
C. identify and define the basic rules for the game of volleyball.
D. utilize verbal communication necessary for effective team work and safe game play.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read "The Evolution of Volleyball" and complete the associated worksheet.
2. Read and review the "Terms of Volleyball" handout focusing on the terms associated with
defensive play. Be prepared to discuss in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a one page essay on your favorite volleyball topic either a coach, player, or team.
2. Write a two page essay on the different types of serves currently used in competitive
volleyball. In your essay, address the techniques, advantages, and disadvantages of each
serve.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Attend a sanctioned volleyball match and a write a short paragraph providing an overview
of the experience.
2. Watch a Division I volleyball match on television and write an overview of the match. In
your overview differentiate between the structure of play in class vs. the structure of play
in the competition that you viewed.
