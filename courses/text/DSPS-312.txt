I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
DSPS 204 - Fundamentals of Reading
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Limitation on Enrollment: Registered with DSPS or Permission of Instructor
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
51 hours Lecture

3 Unit(s)

This reading course is designed for students with intellectual disabilities. Each student will be assessed
at the beginning of the course and will be provided reading instruction that is closely supervised and
individualized based on the results of the assessment.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Demonstrate pre-reading skills.
B. Identify alphabet letters and their sounds.
C. Demonstrate sight vocabulary.
D. Recognize everyday signs and words.
E. Sequence events from a story.
F. Use visual discrimination skills.
G. Apply word attack skills.
H. Use phonics when reading aloud.
I. Use auditory discrimination.
J. Locate concrete answers to story questions.
K. Respond in phrases or sentences to questions.
L. Summarize a short story.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read the story, The Fortune-Teller in your book. Do #3, "Read and Write" on page 55.
2. Read Level III of Word List.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write one paragraph about what you did over the weekend.
2. Do the "Read and Write" on page 63. Change the y to i and add -er to each word.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Write practice words from vocabulary list provided in class today.
2. Go to Lesson 17. On page 97 do the "Read and Write". Put the words under the right
place. Choose town, school or farm.
