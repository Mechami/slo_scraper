I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION

Prerequisite(s): MUS 30
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
34 hours Lecture
68 hours Lab

3 Unit(s)

In this course students will apply their vocal and acting skills in a live performance. Students will also
develop the ability to interrelate with other performers utilizing skills intrinsic to musical productions.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Perform a principal role or perform as part of the ensemble in a musical.
B. Perform necessary body movement and stage deportment techniques.
C. Demonstrate the skills for effective vocal and dramatic interpretation.
D. Follow choreographic direction employed in musical theater production.
E. Apply the make-up necessary for an assigned role within a musical.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read and study the music and lyrics for the current production. Be prepared to discuss the
musical score in your scene.
2. Read the script in the current musical production. Be prepared to discuss your character's
musical numbers and control of your vocal tone.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Attend a play or musical and write a two-page critique of the performance.
2. Write a two-page character analysis for the character you are playing in the current
musical production.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Practice phrasing, breathing, diction and control of your vocal tone for your part in the
production.
2. Using the voice recordings, practice the music and stage movements for your role in the
current musical production. Be prepared to demonstrate your vocal techniques and
understanding of what you are singing about (characteristics behind the song).
