I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
COS 105 - Cosmetology V
Prerequisite(s): COS 104 or Permission of Instructor
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
30 hours Lecture
210 hours Lab

5 Unit(s)

This course is the sixth in a seven-course sequence. Students are introduced to nail diseases and
disorders, manicuring and pedicuring basics and nail tips, wraps and enhancements. Emphasis will be
placed on proper use and disinfection of manicuring tools, manicuring and pedicuring techniques, foot
massage, cleaning and disinfection of foot baths and spas and application, maintenance and removal of
nail tips, wraps and enhancements. Students will continue developing the required skills and
techniques for all aspects of hair care, hair cutting, hair treatments, makeup application, skin care and
nail care and the safe, hygienic practice of Cosmetology procedures. Additionally, students will
continue developing skills in human relations, customer satisfaction and the professional attitudes that
are vital to success in the beauty industry.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Identify and describe basic manicure and pedicure tools and procedures.
B. Explain and apply essential hygienic and infection control principles relating to reusable and
disposable implements.
C. List and demonstrate the massage movements most appropriate for hand and arm massage.
D. Demonstrate correct cleaning and disinfection procedures for nail implements and tools.
E. Demonstrate proper setup for a manicuring table.
F. List and demonstrate the steps needed to mitigate an exposure incident in the salon.
G. Demonstrate all steps for a basic manicure, French manicure and American manicure.
H. Demonstrate the steps used to properly clean and disinfect pedicure baths, whirlpool foot spas
and air-jet basins.
I. Demonstrate appropriate massage movements for foot and leg massage.
J. Demonstrate proper application, repair, maintenance and removal procedures for nail tips and
wraps.
K. Demonstrate proper application, repair, maintenance and removal procedures for monomer
liquid and polymer powder nail enhancements.
L. Demonstrate proper application, repair, maintenance and removal of Ultra Violet (UV) gel nail
enhancements.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read about controversial ingredients used in common cosmetic products that may or may
not cause harm, such as: triclosan, para-aminobenzoic acid, toluene, and formaldehyde. Be
prepared to discuss your findings in class.
2. Read 2 – 3 professional articles that focus on diseases and disorders of the scalp, skin and
nails. Prepare a disease identification guide and share with the class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a 1-2 page essay on current trends in the Cosmetology industry, including hair
design, hair treatments, skin care and salon management.
2. Write a 1-2 page essay summarizing key industry trends for treating acne, wrinkles and
skin discoloration.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Interview five local salon owners focusing on key strategies for passing the state licensing
exam. Summarize your findings.
2. Prepare a poster-size diagram of the structure of the skin. Be prepared to present your
diagram to the class with a summary of how knowledge of the structure of the skin is
critical to skin care and treatment.
