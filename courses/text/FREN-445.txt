I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
FREN 2 - Second Semester French
Prerequisite(s): FREN 1
Recommended Prep: Reading Level III; English Level III
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
68 hours Lecture

4 Unit(s)

This course is the second half of beginning French, which further develops the understanding,
speaking, writing and reading skills. It continues to introduce students to various aspects of the
cultures of French-speaking regions. Students who have completed at least two, but fewer than three
years of high school French (or equivalent) with a letter grade of “A” or “B” within one year of the
present date, should consult the appropriate language instructor before enrolling.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. improve the clarity of pronunciation in French
B. comprehend basic French
C. use both written and oral French for communication in everyday situations
D. formulate arguments supporting the importance of second language acquisition
E. describe elements of French culture

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read a diagram with statistics about recreational activities of French youth and describe
the information to a partner.
2. Read a short text about the climates of different regions of France. Afterward, create a
table with the most important information, such as high temperatures and precipitation.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a brief diary entry about the day's events, in order to practice the past tense.
2. Write a short message to a friend describing the classes you are taking and your favorite
subject.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Complete several "cloze" activities focused on accurate conjugation of verbs located in
the textbook or handout and bring to the next class.
2. Prepare outside of class an original skit based on a communicative context found in course
materials. Perform the skit in class.
