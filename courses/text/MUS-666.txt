I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
MUS 41 - Piano II
Prerequisite(s): MUS 40
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
8.5 hours Lecture
25.5 hours Lab

1 Unit(s)

This course consists of individualized study of the appropriate techniques and repertoire for the piano
at an intermediate level. The focus of the course is on fundamentals: reading music, posture, hand
position, fingering, rhythm, chord structure and progressions, and performance. Emphasis is on the
progressive development of skills needed for solo performance. Achievement is evaluated through a
juried performance. This course is primarily intended for music majors. (C-ID MUS 160).
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Play with precise rhythm at an intermediate level.
B. Play the correct pitches with accurate intonation at an intermediate level.
C. Play with the articulation, dynamics, phrasing, and expression appropriate to the literature being
studied at an intermediate level.
D. Play in a stylistically appropriate manner suitable to the genre, period, and style of the literature.
E. Perform improvised solos as applicable.
F. Demonstrate appropriate blend and balance when performing with other musicians in an
ensemble.
G. Memorize performance literature when appropriate to the performance medium and artistic
tradition.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read Grove's Encyclopedia of Music or any reference work about the piano's history. Be
prepared to discuss in class.
2. Read an issue of "Clavier" online dealing with fingering. Be prepared to discuss in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Prepare a research paper of 1500 words on a prominent piano performer and their
technical contributions.
2. Attend a concert where the piano is the principal instrument or plays a substantial role in
the performance, and write a 2 page paper detailing your experience.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Practice the piano technique of the week for a minimum of 1 hour per day.
2. Listen to a recording of solo piano music. Pay particular attention to the soloist's use of
dynamics, the pedal, and articulation.
