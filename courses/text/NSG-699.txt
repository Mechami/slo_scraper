This course is an exploration of the psychology of maladaptive behavior is explored and students are
introduced to principles and strategies for the promotion of wellness. Graded only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Distinguish indications of mood disturbances and impending crises, and discuss therapeutic
interventions.
B. Describe cultural differences and needs of individuals with mental health disorders.
C. Apply advanced communication strategies to clients experiencing dysfunctional coping
behaviors such as chemical dependency.
D. Describe physical and psychological aspects of wellness.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read in your mental health textbook nursing about implications of medication
administration for the psychiatric/mental health client. Be prepared to discuss this topic in
class.
2. Read in your mental health textbook about the therapeutic relationship. Be prepared to
discuss this topic in small groups in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Complete assigned "Depression" Case Study. Be prepared to discuss this case study in
small groups in class.
2. Complete assigned "Obsessive-Compulsive" Case Study. Be prepared to discuss this case
study in small groups in class.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Complete the worksheet determining your own defense mechanisms. Perform a brief skit
for your classmates regarding this defense mechanism.
2. Complete the Holmes Readjustment scale based on your own recent life experiences. Be
prepared to discuss in class how traumatic life experiences can affect readjustment.
