I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
AJLE 151A - PC 832 Arrest Module
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
28 hours Lecture
12 hours Lab

2 Unit(s)

This course is the first of two modules that comprise the minimum training standard for California
peace officers as specified by the California Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training
(P.O.S.T.). Graduates of this course qualify for minimal peace officer powers as defined by the
California Penal Code. This module contains instruction in 12 specific classroom subjects related to
the authority and limitations of arrest. Additionally, students will participate in 10 hours of hands-on
training in arrest, control, and searching techniques. Pass/No Pass Only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Define professionalism and ethics
B. Identify the components of the criminal justice system
C. Identify the steps of decision making
D. Identify the elements of crime and law
E. Identify the laws of evidence
F. Demonstrate basic investigation and interrogation skills
G. Demonstrate basic communication and report writing skills
H. Demonstrate arrest and control techniques

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read the P.O.S.T. Training and Testing Specifications for each of the 12 presented
subjects.
2. Read the four-volume Workbook Series for the PC 832 Arrest course.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Complete a written arrest report meeting the standards identified in the P.O.S.T. Training
and Testing Specifications.
2. Complete an investigation report meeting the standards identified in the P.O.S.T. Training
and Testing Specifications.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Complete prescribed learning activities contained in the Workbook Series for each of the
12 presented subjects.
2. Practice demonstrated arrest, control, and searching techniques.
