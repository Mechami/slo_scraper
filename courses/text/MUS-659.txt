I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
MUS 22 - Guitar III
Prerequisite(s): MUS 21
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
8.5 hours Lecture
25.5 hours Lab

1 Unit(s)

This course consists of individualized study of the appropriate techniques and repertoire for the guitar
at an advanced intermediate level. The focus of the course is on building technique and repertoire.
Emphasis is on the progressive development of skills needed for solo performance. Achievement is
evaluated through a juried performance. This course is primarily intended for music majors. (C-ID
MUS 160).
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Play with precise rhythm at an advanced intermediate level.
B. Play the correct pitches with accurate intonation at an advanced intermediate level.
C. Play with the articulation, dynamics, phrasing, and expression appropriate to the literature being
studied at an advanced intermediate level.
D. Play in a stylistically appropriate manner suitable to the genre, period, and style of the literature.
E. Perform improvised solos as applicable.
F. Demonstrate appropriate blend and balance when performing as an accompanist.
G. Memorize performance literature when appropriate to the performance medium and artistic
tradition.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read the handout biography of Andres Segovia and be prepared to discuss the technical
contributions that he made to guitar playing.
2. Go online and read several chapters of your choice from "Guitar Technique" by Hector
Quine. Be prepared to discuss what you learn in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Attend a concert of guitar music and write a 2-3 page report that reflects your
understanding of the skills/techniques in the performer's playing, as well as an analysis of
the musical style(s) present.
2. Write a 1500 word research paper about a prominent guitar manufacturer and focus on
how their improvements in the instrument facilitated playing.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Get together with fellow students and practice jamming together. Be prepared to
demonstrate what you have done in class.
2. Practice jazz technique on your instrument a minimum of 8 hours per week.
