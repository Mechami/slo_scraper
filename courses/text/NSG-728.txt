COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
NSG 202 - Acute Care Nurse Assistant
Prerequisite(s): NSG 200, NSG 201 or a Current CNA License
Recommended Prep: Reading Level II; English Level III; Math Level II
Transfer Status: NT
51 hours Lecture
68 hours Lab

4 Unit(s)

This course provides the Certified Nurse Aide (CNA) the knowledge and skills needed to function in
an acute care setting. Topics include Scope of Practice guidelines, effective communication, and
proper procedures for care of patients with a variety of conditions in the acute care setting. Students
will learn about common acute and chronic conditions that require hospitalization and surgical
procedures that patients undergo in the acute care setting. Graded only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Describe the limitations of the acute care nurse assistant with respect to the Scope of Practice
guidelines.
B. Practice effective communication and documentation skills in the acute care setting.
C. Describe knowledge and understanding of a safe and secure environment for patients in the
acute care setting. This includes ensuring the patient's room is free of hazards that might impede
mobility and safety, as well as the provision of physical comfort.
D. Demonstrate and practice safe and effective care of the surgical patient.
E. Collaborate with nursing staff to formulate an appropriate plan of care for the patient with
disorder of the major body systems: gastrointestinal, renal, reproductive, endocrine,
cardiovascular, and respiratory.
F. Identify and follow proper procedures for providing appropriate care for the orthopedic,
oncology, pediatric and/or dying patient.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments

1. Read a professional article on post-operative care of the surgical patient. Be prepared to
discuss in class.
2. Read the chapter in you textbook "Emergency Situations." Be prepared to discuss various
emergency situations and how to deal with them in small groups in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Document your care of a patient in the clinical setting, and turn in in class.
2. Write a paper on a specific surgical procedure, and the care the CNA must provide. Turn
in in class.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Working in a group, prepare an oral presentation to present in class on a common pediatric
disorder and the care the CNA should provide.
2. Interview a patient in the clinical setting about their perception of their care provided by
nursing staff. Be prepared to present in class.
