COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
KIN 41 - Gentle Yoga
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU
17 hours Lecture
34 hours Lab

1.5 Unit(s)

This course introduces students to the art and practice of gentle yoga, using postures which are gentle
in nature and allow a vast majority of the population to perform them. The course includes an
introduction to modified physical postures (asana) which stretch and move the spine and limbs as well
as basic breathing techniques (pranayama), which refers to the expansion of the breath. Students will
learn relaxation techniques for decreasing stress and physical discomforts.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. define terminology specific to yoga practice.
B. explain the different branches of yoga.
C. explain benefits of gentle yoga to health and wellness.
D. demonstrate the complete three part breath.
E. demonstrate asana positions using chairs, blankets, blocks, straps and wall space.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read an article that describes six branches of yoga. Be prepared to discuss in class.
2. Read instructions for relaxation techniques and be prepared to practice in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a brief essay describing the benefits of Yoga to health and wellness. Include how
Yoga can improve your own personal health and well-being.
2. Describe in a brief paper two basic relaxation techniques used in Yoga and how to use
chairs, straps or blocks.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Perform a guided meditation and analyze the process in a short essay.
2. Create a personal practice to use at home based on individual needs.
