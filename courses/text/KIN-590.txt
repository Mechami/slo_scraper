153 hours Lab
This course is designed for students who meet the criteria to compete in intercollegiate softball. All
varsity sports entail practice at designated times. Since skills/proficiencies are enhanced by supervised
repetition and practice, this course may be repeated three times.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. define softball safety and rules.
B. practice the proper etiquette of softball competition.
C. describe offensive and defensive strategies for intercollegiate competition.
D. perform offensive and defensive skills at a level appropriate for intercollegiate competition.
E. demonstrate pre-game, in-game and post-game physical routines.
F. demonstrate proper maintenance of individual equipment and the playing field.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read a minimum of two articles that describe techniques and drills used by collegiate and
Olympic softball coaches and athletes. Be prepared to demonstrate and discuss in class.
2. Research an internet article that outlines the history of Women's Softball and the impact
the sport has had on society. Plan to make a brief presentation in class on your findings.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Maintain a journal each week to review and analyze your game performances.
2. Create a diagram that outlines all defensive position responsibilities and offensive player
tendencies.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. View a softball event and report your observations of the conduct, rules, and techniques.
The event may be viewed in person, through television, or via the internet.
2. Study practice plans, pitching charts, and defensive alignment charts. Apply knowledge to
daily performance.
