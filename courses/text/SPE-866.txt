I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
SPE 312 - Applied Arts for Persons with Substantial Disabilities
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Special Education K-12
Transfer Status: NT
200 hours Lab

0 Unit(s)

This course is designed for persons with substantial disabilities attending a facility in the community
generally known as a day activity center. Applied Arts focuses on learning to utilize a variety of art
and craft media to enhance the creative expression of adults with limited ability to communicate in
traditional ways. Topics will include stained glass, ceramics, beading and jewelry making,
woodworking, and fabric art. An individual assessment of needs as well as an ongoing Individual
Service Plan (ISP) is a component of this course. The ISP will dictate the number of hours each
student will spend in this course. Unlimited repeats. Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory Only. Open
Entry/Open Exit.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Utilize a variety of media to create art and craft objects such as ceramics, stained glass,
woodworking, jewelry, and fiber arts.
B. Demonstrate improved fine and gross motor skills by working with a variety of art and craft
media.
C. Create and select art and craft projects to be shown at local venues.

V. METHODS OF EVALUATION
A. Demonstration and application of individual skills repeated throughout the day to enhance
learning opportunities.
B. Individual Service Plans (ISP) developed to aassess placement of student into program and
establish desired outcomes and individual developmental objectives.
C. Individual progress conferences utilizing frequency charts to evaluate the student's progress
toward established objectives.
VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read instructions for creating a beaded project and apply the knowledge learned to create
a necklace.
2. Read an article about new trends in jewelry to share with classmates.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write steps to reproduce an item. Another classmate will try to recreate the item from the
given steps.
2. Research a stained glass artist. Write a paragraph to share with the class about the artist
you chose.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Not applicable.
