I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
AJLE 164 - POST Dispatcher Course
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
104 hours Lecture
16 hours Lab

6.5 Unit(s)

Becoming a public safety dispatcher means choosing dispatching not only as a career, but as a ethnical
commitment to maintain public trust. Public safety dispatchers must understand their role and
responsibilities, understand the operation of the communications center, and act with a high degree of
professionalism and ethics. This course will cover the communication process of dispatching, criminal
justice system, laws and the California Penal Codes, communication technology operations, resource
referral services, and wellness management. This course meets the minimum requirements for
certification by the California Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training. Pass/No Pass
Only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Describe the basic roles, responsibilities, and duties of the public safety dispatcher within the
law enforcement agencies.
B. Demonstrate practical application of learned information to all newly appointed public safety
dispatchers.
C. Explain the skills and knowledge necessary to work in a law enforcement communications
center in a productive and professional manner.

IV. METHODS OF INSTRUCTION
A. Lecture
B. Videos
C. Real-life Demonstrations
D. Note: Students of Butte College are normally expected to study a minimum of two (2) hours
outside of class for each hour of instruction. However, under the curriculum as set forth by
POST, a minimum of 120 contact hours for this course meets the mandated training
requirements in totality.
E. Proficiency Exercises
F. Audio Recordings
G. Blackboard-based
H. Computer lab-based CAD (Computer Aided Dispatch) Exercise
V. METHODS OF EVALUATION
A. Attendance of a minimum of 120 contact hours as required by POST
B. Participation in dicussions
C. Evaluation assessments and analytical case studies
D. Successful completion of proficiency exercises
E. Successful completion of a 70- question comprehensive written test.
VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Research, review and discuss various case scenarios.
2. The student is expected to read the 13 POST-required learning domains that have been
established as the footprint for this course.
B. Writing Assignments
1. The student will complete writing assignments as directed by the instructor in each
learning domain, combined with written reports made to the class from small group
exercises.
2. Students will be assigned two scenario-based exercises in which they must track
emergency resources in a written report.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. The student will read actual case studies of specific styles and types of public safety
dispatching, as supplied by POST and stored in Blackboard.
2. Students are expected to read each learning domain prior to each class meeting.
