I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
NSG 28 - Role Development
Prerequisite(s): Admission to Vocational Nursing Program
Co-requisite(s): NSG 18, NSG 21, NSG 22, NSG 23 and NSG 24
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU
17 hours Lecture

1 Unit(s)

This course focuses on developing therapeutic communication skills in all aspects of nursing care.
Nursing process as it applies to interpersonal communication and personality development theory will
be integrated throughout course. Graded only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Describe the process and techniques of basic interpersonal communication.
B. Identify three personality development theories and discuss how they effect the communication
process.
C. Utilize the nursing process in communicating with hospitalized clients.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read chapter 42 in Kozier. Come to class prepared to discuss theories of stress disorders.
2. Read Chapter 11 in Shives on therapeutic interactions. Come to class prepared to discuss
therapeutic interactions with hospitalized patients.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Given a set of patient assessment data, organize the data in a body systems format,
Maslow's hierarchy of needs, and functional health patterns. Using information from class
analyze the strengths and weaknesses of each of these formats applied to hospitalized
patients.
2. Complete the case study on role development handed out in class. Identify and develop to
nursing diagnoses with independent and dependent interventions.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Complete the Holmes and Rahe Social Readjustment rating scale.
2. Therapeutic Communication: Process Recording. Using the handout from class write
down verbatim a communication session with a client. Using information from class,
analyze each of your communications.
