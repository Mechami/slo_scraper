I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
NSG 47 - Oncology Nursing
Prerequisite(s): NSG 31, NSG 32, NSG 35, NSG 36 and NSG 37
Co-requisite(s): NSG 41, NSG 42, NSG 43 and NSG 49
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU
34 hours Lecture

2 Unit(s)

This course provides a foundation in the study of oncology nursing care including an introduction to
oncology, prevention, detection and medical treatment. Content includes loss, grief, and death and
dying. Graded only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Describe the psychosocial aspects of stress, loss and grief, death and dying.
B. Utilize the nursing process as applicable to adults experiencing oncology disorders.
C. Compare principles, practices, and skills applicable to the nursing care of adults with oncology
conditions.
D. Describe health maintenance strategies related to oncology conditions.
E. Formulate teaching plans based on health principles and concepts of oncology nursing practice.
F. Describe cultural differences and needs of individuals with oncology conditions.
G. Utilize therapeutic communication strategies in the care of clients and families.
H. Formulate caring interventions when interacting with others.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read in your textbook the content regarding hematologic cancers. Be prepared to discuss
in class.
2. Read in your textbook the chapter regarding the common treatment modalities for breast
cancer. Be prepared to discuss in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Complete assigned chemotherapy care plan. Be prepared to discuss in class.
2. Complete assigned worksheet "Radiation Care Plan". Be prepared to discuss in small
groups in class.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Watch the movie "WIT" with Emma Thompson. Observe the relationship between the
client with cancer and the nurse. Answer the discussion questions and be prepared to
discuss in class.
2. Attend a community resource class for cancer survivors. Be prepared to share reflections
from this class.
