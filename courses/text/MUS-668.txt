I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
MUS 43 - Piano IV
Prerequisite(s): MUS 42
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
8.5 hours Lecture
25.5 hours Lab

1 Unit(s)

This course consists of individualized study of the appropriate techniques and repertoire for the piano
at an advanced level. The focus is on advanced piano techniques, more compositional processes,
ensemble playing and accompanying. Emphasis is on the progressive development of skills needed for
solo performance. Achievement is evaluated through a juried performance. This course is primarily
intended for music majors.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Play with precise rhythm at an advanced level.
B. Play the correct pitches with accurate intonation at an advanced level.
C. Play with the articulation, dynamics, phrasing, and expression appropriate to the literature being
studied at an advanced level.
D. Play in a stylistically appropriate manner suitable to the genre, period, and style of the literature.
E. Perform improvised solos as applicable.
F. Demonstrate appropriate blend and balance when performing as an accompanist or in an
ensemble.
G. Memorize performance literature when appropriate to the performance medium and artistic
tradition.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read the chapter on practice techniques in "The Jazz Piano Book," by Mark Levine. Be
prepared to discuss and demonstrate what you learned in class.
2. Read the chapter on technique in "The Art of Piano Playing," by Heinrich Neuhaus. Be
prepared to discuss in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Research the life of a famous jazz pianist and write a 1500 word summary of their
contributions and importance.
2. Write out a complete harmonic analysis of "Rikki Don't Loose That Number," by Steely
Dan, and be prepared to discuss in class.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Practice technique a minimum of 6 hours per week.
2. Attend a concert where the piano is the main, or featured, instrument and write a 2 page
paper detailing your experience.
