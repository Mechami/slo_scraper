I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
KIN 66 - Softball
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
17 hours Lecture
34 hours Lab

1.5 Unit(s)

This course provides instruction in the fundamental sills of softball, including catching, throwing,
batting, and base running. Students will learn about team play and offensive and defensive strategies.
Emphasis is placed on skill improvement, team cooperation, and safety procedures. Students will
practice skills through game play and structured practice plans.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. explain the basic rules, history, and conduct of softball.
B. discuss team cooperation and proper sportsmanship.
C. identify safety hazards and describe prevention methods in softball.
D. demonstrate basic skills such as throwing, hitting, and catching.
E. describe offensive and defensive strategies.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read a minimum of two articles that outline offensive and defensive skills used in
softball. Be prepared to demonstrate skills in class.
2. Research an internet article that outlines the history of softball and the impact the sport
has had on society. Plan to make a brief presentation in class on your findings.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a brief paper summarizing the responsibilities of each position in softball.
2. Write an essay that reviews and analyzes your last three practice performances.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Research potential safety hazards in softball and what can be done to prevent injuries. Plan
to discuss your findings in class.
2. View a softball event and report your observations as they address
conduct, rules, and techniques.
