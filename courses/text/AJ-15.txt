I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
AJ 6 - Evidence
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Reading Level IV; English Level III
Transfer Status: CSU
51 hours Lecture

3 Unit(s)

This course examines categories of evidence and legal rules governing its admission and exclusion in
the criminal process. (C-ID AJ 124). Graded only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Identify and differentiate various types of evidence.
B. Define and describe key rules of evidence.
C. Critically evaluate and apply the rules of evidence to specific case facts.
D. Prepare legal briefs of appellate course decisions.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS

A. Reading Assignments
1. Read six appellate court decisions and be prepared to discuss in class.
2. Read the chapter on qualification of witnesses. Review the law, paying particular
attention to the legal standards for witness testimony. Come to class prepared to discuss
the legal necessity for competent witness testimony both conceptually and as it relates to a
fair trial.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Read the chapter on cross examination. Prepare a one-two page legal brief of a related
appellate court decision and orally present in class.
2. Conduct legal research and define specified theories and terminology used in the judicial
system.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Define and summarize instructor provided vocabulary related to evidence within the
judicial system.
2. Identify six constitutional legal issues that relate to written statutes and in an essay format
summarize each.
