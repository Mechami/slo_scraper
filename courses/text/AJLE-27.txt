I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
AJLE 141 - Law Enforcement Academy; Fish and Game Emphasis
Prerequisite(s): (See Catalog for Program Admission Criteria)
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
676 hours Lecture
540 hours Lab

50 Unit(s)

This 30-week course prepares the student for a career as a peace officer with an emphasis on the
Department of Fish and Game (DFG), under the authority of the California Commission on Peace
Officer Standards and Training (P.O.S.T.). The curriculum covers 42 learning domains ranging from
academic subjects on criminal law, investigation and patrol procedures to psychomotor skills on
defensive tactics, firearms training, driving skills and officer-violator contacts. The successful
completion of this course will allow the student to be employed as a peace officer in California (valid
for three years after graduation) and lead to the issuance of a Basic Law Enforcement Certificate from
P.O.S.T. after one year of approved field service at a law enforcement agency. Graded only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Demonstrate competency in 42 P.O.S.T. learning domains.
B. Demonstrate, through written exams, crime scene scenario testing, and manipulative skills, core
competencies as mandated by P.O.S.T.
C. Demonstrate, through written exams, DFG scenario testing, and manipulative skills, core
competencies as mandated by DFG.
