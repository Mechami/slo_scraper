I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
DRAM 10 - Rehearsal and Performance for Musical Theatre
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Co-requisite(s): DRAM 19
Limitation on Enrollment: Selected by audition
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU
153 hours Lab

3 Unit(s)

This course provides instruction and supervised participation in musical theatre rehearsal and
performance. Students enrolled in this course will be formed into a company to present a musical
production. Each student will participate in a theatrical production in the style of theatre generally
termed American Musical Comedy.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Identify the professional responsibilities of a producing group associated with the audition,
preparation, and performance of a musical theatre production before an audience.
B. Demonstrate movement, choreography and blocking in a musical production.
C. Identify and demonstrate the proper use of voice, breathing, articulation, projection, and pitch.
D. Recognize and demonstrate character and stage presence.
E. Identify and describe production tasks and individual responsiblities as part of a production
ensemble.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read the script and musical score of current musical production and create a score of the
physical actions. Be prepared to share your score in class.
2. Read the libretto and listen to the Broadway Cast CD from which the student duet
scene/song is taken. Be prepared to discuss in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a 2 page evaulation on the current production after its close focusing on acting,
movement, staging and choreography.
2. Write a minimum 2 page show and scene character analysis based on the outline provided
by the instructor.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Attend a musical theatre performance in the community or at a professional level. Write a
play critique and present to the class.
2. View one dramatic theatre production and one musical theatre production on video. Be
prepared to explain how the acting style differs between the two.
