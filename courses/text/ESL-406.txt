COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
ESL 256 - ESL Grammar & Usage Workshop
3 Unit(s)
Prerequisite(s): Qualifying eligibility for ESL Level IV based on ESL assessment or
ESL 313
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
51 hours Lecture
This course improves students' ability to use Standard English grammar. Emphasis will be placed on
increasing student understanding of various grammatical structures and sentence mechanics in
preparation for academic reading and writing. Pass/No Pass Only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Identify grammar trouble spots.
B. Recognize and correct common grammatical errors involving gerunds, infinitives and phrasal
verbs.
C. Recognize and correct sentence boundary errors, subject/verb agreement errors, article errors,
and errors associated with adjectives and adverbs.
D. Identify the basic parts of speech.
E. Correctly punctuate simple and compound sentences.
F. Utilize a variety of strategies to improve spelling and punctuation.
G. Recognize the difference between spoken and written English.
H. Compose grammatically correct sentences and paragraphs in a variety of tenses.
