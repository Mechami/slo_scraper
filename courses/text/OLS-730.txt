I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
OLS 323 - Occupational and Academic Assessment
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
80 hours Lab

0 Unit(s)

This course prepares students for placement in the Occupational and Life Skills Certificate Program.
The focus of this course is to assess individual abilities, determine avenues of interest, identify
vocational and academic goals, validate academic skill levels, and establish a student vocational
contract. Topics include vocational aptitude, learning styles, decision making, and personal values.
Unlimited repeats. Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory Only. Open Entry/Open Exit.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Recognize their individual area of vocational and academic strengths.
B. Identify their vocational and/or academic goals to attain employment.
C. Identify their career path of interest.
D. Contribute to the development of his/her own student vocational contract.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Select and read an article from a trade journal of interest to you and be prepared to discuss
what you learned in class.
2. Read the passage provided by the instructor, highlight and look up definitions of words
you do not understand. Submit the definitions with the instructor.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a paragraph about your vocational goals and submit it to your instructor.
2. Write a paragraph about your dream job and turn it in to the instructor.
