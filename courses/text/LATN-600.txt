I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
LATN 3 - Third Semester Latin
Prerequisite(s): LATN 2 with a grade of "C" or higher, or equivalent
Recommended Prep: Reading Level IV; English Level IV
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
68 hours Lecture

4 Unit(s)

This course develops proficient knowledge of Latin pronunciation, vocabulary, composition, syntax
and especially grammatical constructions using subjunctive verbs. This course requires the study of
Latin influence on the English language and Roman cultural influence on modern civilization.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Clearly and confidently pronounce Latin correctly.
B. Analyze form, identify, and use verbs correctly in the subjunctive mood.
C. Speak Latin at an advanced level using complex sentences (e.g. purpose clauses, indirect
statements).
D. Write Latin in the indicative and subjunctive moods at the intermediary level and beyond.
E. Recognize Latin words in both colloquial and technical English, therefore expanding one's
vocabulary.
F. Connect their knowledge of Roman history and culture to contemporary society and culture.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1.
1. Read the essay on Roman military weapons and tactics on pages 16-23 in the textbook.
Imagine that you are in the Roman infantry in formation with an enemy army advancing
toward you. Describe how you employ your weapons and armor as the battle begins.
2. Memorize the dialog for one of the characters in the Fabella (short skit) on page 21 of
the textbook. Prepare to perform the skit with two of your classmates.
B. Writing Assignments
1.
Read the Latin story about the Roman Civil War on page 30 of the textbook. Translate
the first two paragraphs into formal English. Answer the comprehension questions (in
Latin) at the end of the story.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1.
1. Read the poem on pages 49-50 of the textbook until you well understand the meaning.
Recite the poem aloud several times until you not only pronounce and phrase the lines

correctly, and also convey both the rhythm and the emotional content of the poem. Then
record your recitation on the course web site or as a voice mail.
2. Do the translation exercises on pages 158-159 in the textbook. In section three, indicate
what type of subjunctive is used (exhortation, command, optative (wish), deliberative
question or indirect question).
