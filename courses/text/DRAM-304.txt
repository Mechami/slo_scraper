COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
DRAM 14 - Theatre Production Workshop
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
153 hours Lab

3 Unit(s)

Students will gain practical experience in the application of production responsibilities in any of the
following: stage management, house management, construction, scenery, properties, costume,
lighting, sound, and running crews. (C-ID THTR 192).
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Demonstrate proficiency in the skills required for a technical theatre crew.
B. Demonstrate and employ basic skills to address the technical demands of a theatrical production.
C. Execute assigned duties and responsibilities in technical rehearsals, during production run, and
strike.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read the chapter on Painting scenery and be prepared to discuss the methods of painting
and brushes and use of related equipment in live theatre.
2. Read the chapter on Constructing Scenery and be prepared to discuss the types of scenery,
framed and textured, and sculptured surfaces used in live theatre.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Critique theatre productions performed during the semester in relationship to the scenery
and the performers.
2. Write an evaluation of the production process of the specific play encountered.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Visit other theatres in the area, e.g. CSUC Theatre shop, Blue Room Theatre, Chico
Theatre Company etc.
2. Attend a specific play and be prepared to write an evaluation or discuss the performance
in class.
