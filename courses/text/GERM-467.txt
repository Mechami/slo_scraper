I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
GERM 1 - First Semester German
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Reading Level III; English Level III
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
68 hours Lecture

4 Unit(s)

This course is a study of beginning German emphasizing grammar, reading, writing, speaking, and
listening comprehension. It also introduces the student to various aspects of German culture. Students
who have completed at least one, but fewer than two years of high school German (or equivalent)with
a letter grade of “A” or “B” within one year of the present date, should register for Second Semester
German.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. recognize and begin to imitate German pronunciation
B. comprehend basic German at the introductory level
C. speak basic German at the introductory level
D. write basic German at the introductory level
E. formulate arguments supporting the importance of second language acquisition
F. describe elements of Germanic culture

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read a diagram with statistics about recreational activities of German youth and describe
the information to a partner.
2. Read a short text about the climates of different regions of Germany. Afterwards create a
table with the most important information, such as high temperatures and precipitation.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a brief diary entry about the day's events, in order to practice the "Perfekt" past
tense.
2. Write a short message to a friend describing the classes you are taking and your favorite
subject.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Prepare outside of class an original skit based on a communicative context found in course
materials. Perform the skit in class.
2. Complete several "cloze" activities located in the textbook or handout bring to the
following class.
