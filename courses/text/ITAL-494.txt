I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
ITAL 2 - Second Semester Italian
Prerequisite(s): ITAL 1
Recommended Prep: Reading Level III; English Level III
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
68 hours Lecture

4 Unit(s)

This course is the second half of beginning Italian, which further develops the skills of understanding,
speaking, reading and writing Italian. It continues to introduce students to various aspects of Italian
culture. Students who have completed at least two, but fewer than three years of high school Italian (or
equivalent) with a letter grade of "A" or "B" within one year of the present date, should consult the
appropriate language instructor before enrolling.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. improve the clarity of pronunciation in Italian
B. comprehend basic Italian
C. use both written and oral Italian for communication in everyday situations
D. formulate arguments supporting the importance of second language acquisition
E. appraise elements of Italian culture

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read a diagram with statistics about recreational activities of Italian youth and describe
the information to a partner.
2. Read a short text about the climates of different regions of Italy. Afterward, create a table
with the most important information, such as high temperatures and precipitation.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a brief diary entry about the day's events, in order to practice the past tense.
2. Write a short message to a friend describing the classes you are taking and your favorite
subject.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Complete several "cloze" activities focused on accurate conjugation of verbs located in
the textbook or handout and bring to the next class.
2. Prepare outside of class an original skit based on a communicative context found in course
materials. Perform the skit in class.
