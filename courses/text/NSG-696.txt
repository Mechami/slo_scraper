This course is a continuation of hospital clinical experience with a concentration on adult
Medical-Surgical Nursing. Pass/No Pass Only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Provide client's basic needs for personal hygeine, comfort, and safety in the setting of a
therapeutic environment, while observing patient's rights.
B. Perform nursing procedures required to meet the client's basic needs.
C. Asess, plan, implement, and record patient care.
D. Administer medications safely.
E. Follow legal standards of practice.
F. Instruct the client regarding health maintenance.
G. Consistently maintain principles of infection control in the clinical setting.
H. Present and maintain a professional demeanor.
I. Utilize basic communication skills to work effectively with health team members to provide
individualized and culturally appropriate nursing care.
J. Maintain client confidentiality according to HIPPA and institutional regulations.
K. Assume leadership roles in the clinical setting.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Review Leifer prior to obstetrics or pediatric rotation to better understand disease
processes and nursing care of pediatric and obstetric clients while considering the
developmental and cultural factors. Be prepared to discuss these topics in
post-conference.
2. Read Lewis,to research disease processes, pathology, treatment, and nursing care to
facilitate better understanding of care for specific clients in the clinical setting. Be
prepared to discuss this topic in post-conference.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Complete a pediatric journal reflecting on observational experiences after each day of
clinical experiences in a pediatric setting. Be prepared to discuss this in small groups at
post conference.
2. Complete leadership worksheets after performing each of the three roles in the leadership
team. Be prepared to discuss this at clinical post-conference.
