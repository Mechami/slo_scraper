COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
AJLE 142 - Supplemental Law Enforcement Academy; Fish and Game
Emphasis
Prerequisite(s): Must be employed with a law enforcement agency and have
already successfully completed the P.O.S.T. Regular Basic Course and possess a
Basic Law Enforcement Certificate from P.O.S.T.
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
210 hours Lecture
111 hours Lab

14.5 Unit(s)

This 8-week course prepares the student for a career as a peace officer with the Department of Fish
and Game (DFG). The curriculum includes a review of academic subjects on criminal law,
investigation and patrol procedures to psychomotor skills on defensive tactics, firearms training,
driving skills and officer-violator contacts. Primary focus will be on topics directly related to the
operation of the Department of Fish and Game, Law Enforcement Division and performing the typical
duties of a game warden. Graded only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Meet or exceed the requirements as set forth by P.O.S.T.
B. Demonstrate, through written exams, scenario testing, and manipulative skills, core
competencies as mandated by P.O.S.T.
C. Demonstrate, through written exam, DFG scenario testing, and manipulative skills, core
competencies as mandated by DFG.
D. Demonstrate, through written exams, scenario testing, and manipulative skills, core
competencies as mandated by P.O.S.T.
E. Demonstrate, through written exams, scenario testing, and manipulative skills, core
competencies as mandated by P.O.S.T.
