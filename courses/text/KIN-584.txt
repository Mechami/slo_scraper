153 hours Lab
This course is designed for students who meet the criteria to compete in intercollegiate baseball. All
varsity sports entail practice and games at designated times. Since skills/proficiencies are enhanced by
supervised repetition and practice, this course may be repeated three times.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. define baseball safety and rules.
B. practice the proper etiquette of baseball competition.
C. describe offensive and defensive strategies for intercollegiate competition.
D. perform offensive and defensive skills at a level appropriate for intercollegiate competition.
E. demonstrate pre-game, in-game and post-game physical routines.
F. demonstrate proper maintenance of individual equipment and the playing field.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Research an internet article that outlines the history of baseball and the impact the sport
has had on society. Plan to make a brief presentation in class on your findings.
2. Read a minimum of two articles that outline offensive and defensive skills used in
baseball. Be prepared to demonstrate skills in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write an essay that reviews and analyzes your last three game performances.
2. Write a brief paper summarizing the responsibilities of each position in baseball.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. View a Major League Baseball game and report your observations as they address
conduct, rules, and techniques.
2. Study practice plans, pitching charts, and defensive alignment charts. Apply knowledge to
daily performance.
