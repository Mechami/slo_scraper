I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
FSC 6 - Fire Tactics and Strategy
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Reading Level III; English Level III
Transfer Status: CSU
51 hours Lecture

3 Unit(s)

This course introduces students to the principles of fire control in municipal and suburban
environments. It includes an overview of the utilization of human resources, equipment and
extinguishing agents on the fireground.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Explain the goals of municipal and suburban firefighting.
B. Analyze priorities in responding to municipal and suburban firefighting situations.
C. Identify and prioritize basic tactical concepts and procedures in municipal and suburban
firefighting.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Locate and read an article regarding a municipal fire where a fatality occurred, and be
prepared to discuss the relationship between tactics used and the fatality in class.
2. Read an instructor provided fire scenario, and be prepared to discuss a company-based
attack of the fire.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a one page pre-plan of a commercial building, and identify tactics to be utilized in
response to a fire in that building.
2. Write a two page paper describing an assigned interview with a fire service company
officer and considerations in making tactical fireground decisions.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Conduct a one-on-one interview with a fire company officer, and discuss prioritization of
fire response strategies. Be prepared to share what you learned in class.
2. Tour a commercial building, and be prepared to discuss a tactical fire response plan for
that building.
