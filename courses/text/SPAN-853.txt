I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
SPAN 1 - First Semester Spanish
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Reading Level III; English Level III
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
68 hours Lecture

4 Unit(s)

This course is a study of beginning Spanish emphasizing grammar, reading, writing and speaking. It
also introduces the student to various aspects of the cultures of Spanish-speaking regions. Students
who have completed at least one, but fewer than two years of high school Spanish (or equivalent) with
a letter grade of "A" or "B" within one year of the present date, should register for Second Semester
Spanish.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. recognize and begin to imitate Spanish pronunciation
B. comprehend basic Spanish at the introductory level
C. speak basic Spanish at the introductory level
D. write basic Spanish at the introductory level
E. formulate argument supporting the importance of second language acquisition
F. appraise elements of Hispanic cultures

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read a diagram with statistics about recreational activities of Spanish youth and describe
the information to a partner.
2. Read a short text about the climates of different regions of Peru. Afterward, create a table
with the most important information, such as high temperatures and precipitation.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a brief diary entry about the day's events, in order to practice the past tense.
2. Write a short message to a friend describing the classes you are taking and your favorite
subject.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Complete several "cloze" activities focused on accurate conjugation of verbs located in
the textbook or handout and bring to the next class.
2. Prepare outside of class an original skit based on a communicative context found in course
materials. Perform the skit in class.
