COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
EH 44 - Wine Vineyard Management
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: Reading Level III; English Level III; Math Level III
Transfer Status: CSU
51 hours Lecture

3 Unit(s)

This course is designed for students to learn the responsibilities of vineyard management including
diagnosis and correction of problems, vineyard development, financial projections and budgeting,
labor contracting, labor supervision, and crop sale contracts as they relate to wine grapes.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Develop an annual plan of operations for a vineyard including financial plans, budgets and
scheduling of agricultural practices.
B. Demonstrate understanding of the process for establishing a vineyard such as locating sources
for materials and labor.
C. Define the principles used in hiring, training and supervising employees.
D. Analyze vineyard problems and develop solutions including doing research for viticulture
information.
E. Relate the operations and practices in the vineyard to the desired outcome of the winemaker.
III. COURSE CONTENT
A. General Goals
1. This course will introduce the student to annual planning of operations for a vineyard
including establishing a vineyard, annual cultural practices, recognizing vineyard
problems and discussing applicable solutions and vineyard/winery relations.
