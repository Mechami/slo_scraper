COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
FASH 10 - Clothing Construction II
Prerequisite(s): FASH 9
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU
34 hours Lecture
51 hours Lab

3 Unit(s)

This is an intermediate course in clothing construction. This course emphasizes construction of a
capsule wardrobe focusing on a variety of novelty fashion fabrics. The course emphasizes personal
fitting and custom dressmaking utilizing intermediate sewing techniques.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Construct garments using commercial patterns and novelty fabrics.
B. Recognize and apply the principles of proper professional sewing techniques.
C. Demonstrate clothing construction techniques at an intermediate level.
D. Demonstrate proper sewing techniques for different garment styles and fabrics.
E. Evaluate the causes of various fitting problems and correct them using appropriate alteration
techniques.

IV. METHODS OF INSTRUCTION

hour of lecture
B. Lab Participation
C. Lecture/Demonstration
D. Media Presentations
V. METHODS OF EVALUATION
A. Quizzes
B. Garment Construction
C. Technique Book (Samples) with instructions
D. Participation in Lab
VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read the section in your text on sleeve treatments. Be prepared to identify sleeve
treatments in class.
2. Read an article about garment alteration and use the techniques you learn to troubleshoot
the fit of your project.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a 1-2 page summary of various pocket treatments and evaluate the different
applications of these treatments.
2. Prepare a 2-3 page journal entry about your experiences with novelty fashion fabrics.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Locate an online tutorial on a sewing technique that you are having trouble with. Be
prepared to share what you learn with the class.
2. Add to the binder of construction techniques that you started in FASH 9. Focus on

intermediate to advanced techniques.
