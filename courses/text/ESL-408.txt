I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
ESL 300 - American Citizenship
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
51 hours Lecture

0 Unit(s)

This course will provide non-U.S. citizens with an in-depth study of the federal and state system of
government and the U.S. Constitution, preparing them for citizenship. The course will include civil
rights, voting, historical development of the U.S., types of government, the electoral process, political
parties, the executive, legislative, and judicial branches of government, and the relationship between
the state and federal systems of government. Unlimited repeats. Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory Only.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Recognize a map of the United States and have a familiarity with the fifty states.
B. Recall major incidents of US discovery and colonizations
C. List and discuss the duties of the three branches of the national government.
D. Identify the various agencies of state and local government.
E. Identify critical elements of the US Constitution and Bill of Rights.
F. Apply their voting and civil rights.
G. Follow procedures and complete all forms of the INS necessary to become a US citizens.
