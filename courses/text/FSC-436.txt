I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
FSC 18 - Basic Wildland Firefighting
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU
51 hours Lecture

3 Unit(s)

This course prepares students to seek employment as wildland firefighters. It provides students with
fundamental knowledge of wildland fire behavior, control, prevention and firefighter safety. Students
will learn suppression and survival methods and techniques, and the use of hand and power tools and
burning devices in the suppression of wildland fires. Upon successful completion of this course,
students will receive California State Fire Marshall (CSFM) certificates of completion for the
following National Wildland Fire Training courses: S-110 Basic Wildland Fire Orientation; S-130
Firefighter Training; S-133 Look Up, Look Down, Look Around; S-190 Introduction to Wildland Fire
Behavior; I-100 Introduction to Incident Command System.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Describe principles of wildland fire control and the role of a firefighter under wildland fire
control operations.
B. Interpret wildland fire behavior conditions and select appropriate fireline safety precautions.
C. Analyze fire behavior, and develop an incident action plan with consideration for fire behavior
facts and physics, and utilizing firefighting strategies.
D. Evaluate fireline conditions and select the appropriate extinguishing method available.
E. Recognize unsafe working environments and establish methods to rectify them.
F. Prepare a fire service employment application package, including a cover letter, an application
and a resume.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Locate and read an article about a serious fire where a fatality occurred, and be prepared to
discuss in class.
2. Read a chapter in the textbook related to firefighter safety, and be prepared to discuss in
class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Prepare a minimum one page employment application cover letter and instructor-provided
sample applications for employment.
2. Prepare an incident action plan for a fire emergency, and be prepared to present in class.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Interview a career firefighter, and prepare responses to 20 instructor provided questions
related to the interview.
2. Utilize various government websites to locate firefighter employment opportunities, and
be prepared to discuss your results in class.
