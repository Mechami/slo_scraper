I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
GERM 4 - Fourth Semester German
Prerequisite(s): GERM 3
Recommended Prep: Reading Level III; English Level III
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
68 hours Lecture

4 Unit(s)

This course is the second half of intermediate German, which further develops language skills:
reading, writing, listening comprehension and speaking. Through continued study of German language
and culture students further increase their fluency and grammatical accuracy in the four skill areas in
expanded written analysis and discussion of both modern and classical German texts.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. pronounce German correctly, with increased ease and clarity
B. comprehend both written and spoken German at the upper-intermediate level
C. use both written and oral German at the upper-intermediate level for communication in
everyday situations and several specialized topic areas, e.g. contemporary social issues and
German history
D. formulate arguments supporting the importance of second language acquisition
E. compare elements of Germanic culture to the United States

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read an assigned excerpt of German literature in the target language, in preparation for a
class discussion. Read the text three times outside of class.
2. Scan a German newspaper for job postings. By identifying the qualifications and hiring
process, be prepared for a cross-cultural discussion about hiring practices in the USA and
Germany.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a summary of the main events in a piece of German literature.
2. Edit and revise essays written by peers. Focus will be set on clarity, grammatical
accuracy, and the revision process.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Prepare outside of class an original skit based on a communicative context found in course
materials. The skit will then be performed in class.
2. Watch several German films and write a summary/critique in German.
