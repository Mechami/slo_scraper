COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
DRAM 19 - Choreography for Performance
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Co-requisite(s): DRAM 10
Limitation on Enrollment: Selected by audition
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
17 hours Lecture
51 hours Lab

2 Unit(s)

This course introduces students to dance styles and choreography used in theatrical performance.
Students will learn appropriate warm-up, technique for dance, and strengthening exercises. Topics
will include the rules of etiquette and basic terminology used in theatre and dance production.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. Define the rules of etiquette for dance in theatre.
B. Identify and demonstrate the styles of choreography and movement used in theatre.
C. Perform in dance and musical numbers in theatrical productions.
D. Demonstrate improved dance technique.
E. Perform a dance number showing correct timing, technique and style.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read an article describing techniques used to design and choreograph a dance
routine. Be prepared to perform a short example.
2. Read and study the script for the current production. Be prepared to discuss character
development, plot and scenes in class.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Outline the choreography being taught for the current production. The outline should
include each eight count of movement, the steps to be performed, and entrances and exits.
2. Write a two page character analysis for a character in the current production.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Research the style of dance being performed in the current production. Be prepared to
discuss and perform the dance in class.
2. Pair up with a partner and analyze and critique each other's routine. Share your
observations with one another.
