COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
KIN 58 - Badminton
Prerequisite(s): NONE
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: CSU/UC
17 hours Lecture
34 hours Lab

1.5 Unit(s)

This course provides instruction in the fundamental skills of badminton. Students will learn all primary
shots including serves, overhead shots, underhand shots, and drop shots. Skills, etiquette, court
strategy, and rules will be applied through game play.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. define and identify the rules and strategy of badminton.
B. demonstrate improvement in the fundamental techniques of badminton such as serve, overhead
clear, drop, and smash shots.
C. demonstrate proper sportsmanship through game etiquette.
D. identify and analyze overall game play proficiency through peer observation.

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Research the history of badminton. Write a short essay about where badminton originated
and when it came to the United States.
2. Read about badminton basics on the USA Badminton website. Be prepared to share
something interesting you learned.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Research differences between European and American strategies of game play. Write a
two-page essay outlining the differences.
2. Write a one-page summary of the official rules of badminton pertaining to the shuttle and
the racquet.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. View any type of badminton competition to analyze strategies of game play which can
include competitions and/or documentaries.
2. Prepare a mock tournament outlining rules, brackets, and game personnel. Present a 5
minute presentation to the class.
