COURSE OUTLINE
I. CATALOG DESCRIPTION
ITAL 101 - Intermediate Conversational Italian
Prerequisite(s): ITAL 100
Recommended Prep: NONE
Transfer Status: NT
51 hours Lecture

3 Unit(s)

This course emphasizes the development of basic/intermediate understanding and speaking skills in
Italian. Minimal emphasis is placed on formal grammar. Multimedia cultural presentations are offered
throughout the course. This course is intended for those who wish to continue development of
"survival" Italian in everyday situations.
II. OBJECTIVES
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
A. demonstrate an understanding of Italian as spoken in informal situations at a basic/intermediate
level
B. demonstrate the ability to communicate orally in Italian in informal situations at a
basic/intermediate level
C. compare and contrast elements of Italian culture

VI. EXAMPLES OF ASSIGNMENTS
A. Reading Assignments
1. Read a diagram with statistics about recreational activities of Italy youth and describe the
information to a partner.
2. Read a short text about the climates of different regions of Italian. Afterward, create a
table with the most important information, such as high temperatures and precipitation.
B. Writing Assignments
1. Write a brief diary entry about the day's events, in order to practice the past tense.
2. Write a short message to a friend describing the classes you are taking and your favorite
subject.
C. Out-of-Class Assignments
1. Complete several "cloze" activities focused on accurate conjugation of verbs located in
the textbook or handout and bring to the next class.
2. Prepare outside of class an original skit based on a communicative context found in course
materials. Perform the skit in class.
